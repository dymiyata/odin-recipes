Garlic Noodles

Description

Garlic noodles are a flavorful fusion dish with origins rooted in the culinary creativity of San Francisco’s Vietnamese immigrant community. They were popularized in the 1970s by a Vietnamese-American restaurant, Thanh Long, which brought Southeast Asian flavors into a Western dining context. Originally created as a complement to the restaurant’s signature roasted crab, garlic noodles quickly became iconic in their own right. Blending Vietnamese, Chinese, and Italian influences, this dish exemplifies the adaptation of Asian cuisine for American palates. While its precise origins are tied to the Vietnamese diaspora, garlic noodles have since become beloved across various Asian-American kitchens, evolving with different regional takes and variations.

Their distinct blend of buttery richness and umami with a punch of garlic encapsulates the multicultural essence of Vietnamese-American cooking. Today, garlic noodles are a staple in many restaurants, especially along the West Coast, and are celebrated as a simple yet indulgent dish that bridges diverse culinary traditions.

Ingredients

  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 20 medium garlic gloves (smashed in mortar and pestle)
  • 4 teaspoons oyster sauce
  • 2 teaspoons shoyu
  • 2 teaspoons fish sauce
  • 1 pound dried spaghetti
  • 1 ounce Parmesan
  • sliced scallions (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Melt the butter in a wok or saucepan over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook, stirring, until fragrant but not browned, about 2 minutes. Add the oyster sauce, soy sauce and fish sauce, and stir to combine. Remove from the heat.

  2. Meanwhile, bring 1½ inches of water to a boil in a 12-​inch skillet or sauté pan over high heat. (Alternatively, heat up just enough water to cover the spaghetti in a large Dutch oven or saucepan.) Add the pasta, stir a few times to make sure it’s not clumping, and cook, stirring occasionally, until just shy of al dente (about 2 minutes short of the recommended cook time on the package).

  3. Using tongs, transfer the cooked pasta to the garlic sauce, along with whatever water clings to it. (Reserve the pasta water in the skillet.) Increase the heat to high, add the cheese to the wok, and stir with a wooden spatula or spoon and toss vigorously until the sauce is creamy and emulsified, about 30 seconds. If the sauce looks too watery, let it keep reducing. If it looks greasy, splash some more cooking water into it and let it re-​emulsify. Stir in the scallions (if using), and serve immediately.
