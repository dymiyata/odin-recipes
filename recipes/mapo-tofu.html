Mapo Tofu

Description

Mapo tofu is a classic Chinese dish from the Sichuan province, known for its bold flavors and characteristic numbing spiciness. It features tender cubes of silken or medium-firm tofu simmered in a rich, spicy sauce made from doubanjiang (fermented broad bean paste) and seasoned with Sichuan peppercorns, which provide a unique, tingling sensation. The dish also includes ground pork or beef, which adds depth and umami, along with garlic, ginger, green onions, and sometimes fermented black beans for an extra layer of complexity. The result is a vibrant red and spicy sauce that perfectly balances heat, saltiness, and savory flavors. Mapo tofu is traditionally served hot, usually with steamed rice, allowing the intense flavors to shine.

Ingredients

  • oil
  • 1-2 Thai bird chili peppers (sliced)
  • 6-8 dried red chilies (roughly chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon of Sichuan peppercorns (ground)
  • 3 tablespoons ginger (minced)
  • 3 tablespoons garlic (minced)
  • 8 ounces ground pork
  • 1-2 tablespoons of Pixian doubanjiang
  • 2/3 cup chicken broth (or water)
  • 1 pound silken tofu
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/2 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 scallion (sliced)

Steps

  1. First, we toast the chilies. Heat your wok or a small saucepan over low heat. Add half of the oil and throw in the fresh and dried peppers. Stir occasionally and heat until fragrant, about 5 minutes, ensuring that the peppers don’t burn. Remove from heat and set aside.

  2. Heat the remaining half of the oil in your wok over medium heat. Add the ginger. After 1 minute, add the garlic. Fry for another minute, and then turn up the heat to high and add the ground pork. Break up the meat and fry it until it’s cooked through. Add your ground Sichuan peppercorns and stir for about 15-30 seconds, taking care to not let it burn, as it will turn bitter if it does.

  3. Add the doubanjiang to the mixture and stir it in well. Add the chicken broth to the wok and stir. Let this simmer for a minute or so. While that's happening, ready your tofu and combine the water and cornstarch in a small bowl.

  4. Add the cornstarch mixture to your sauce and stir. Let it bubble away until the sauce starts to thicken.

  5. Then add your chili oil from before—peppers and all! Stir the oil into the sauce, and add the tofu. Use your spatula to gently toss the tofu in the sauce. Let everything cook for 3-5 minutes. Add the sesame oil and sugar (if using) along with the scallions and stir until the scallions are just wilted.

  6. Serve with a last sprinkle of Sichuan peppercorn powder.
